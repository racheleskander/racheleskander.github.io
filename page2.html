Enclosed Space Control

The objective of the project was to create a secure and controlled environment for medication storage by developing an enclosed safe space equipped with advanced sensors. The implementation involves integrating a human presence and motion sensor and an accelerometer sensor within a compact box, which is strategically placed inside a medicine cabinet. The primary goal is to detect and prevent unauthorized access, particularly by children, to the medication. This innovative solution aims to enhance safety in environments like patient homes, doctor offices, and pharmacies, mitigating the risk of accidental ingestion or misuse of drugs by implementing proactive measures through sensor technology.

We received a patent to begin our project efforts.
Revision 1: Low resolution prototype. We used foamcore, felt, and elastic to create this revision
Revision 2: Clip Closed. This revision encloses the NG tube, while having a hinging mechanism to open, a clasp to secure the hinge, and sew holes to attach to the headgear. It was made with Polylactic Acid filament using a Fused Filament Fabrication method.
